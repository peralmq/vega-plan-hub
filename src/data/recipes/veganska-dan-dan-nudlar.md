## Ingredients

- 500 g udon noodles
- 5 scallions (both white and green parts)
- 1 tbsp Sichuan peppercorns
- 300 g vegan mince (sojafärs)
- 2 tsp sesame oil
- 1.5 + 3 tbsp Japanese soy sauce (divided)
- Rapeseed oil, for frying
- 6 garlic cloves
- 3 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 1 fresh red chili (or 0–2 tbsp sambal oelek)
- 3 tbsp sauerkraut
- 2.5 tbsp chili oil
- 3 tbsp Chinese vinegar
- 1 tbsp agave syrup
- 2 tbsp tahini

## Instructions

1. Prepare the vegetables: slice the scallions, separating the white and green parts. Grate the ginger and garlic. Chop the sauerkraut and slice the chili if using.
2. Cook the noodles according to package instructions. Rinse in cold water and set aside until serving.
3. Toast the Sichuan peppercorns in a dry wok or pan until fragrant. Remove and crush in a mortar. Set aside for topping.
4. Mix the sauce ingredients in a bowl: 3 tbsp soy sauce, chili oil, tahini, agave syrup, and vinegar.
5. Heat a wok or large pan over high heat with a little rapeseed oil. Fry the vegan mince, sauerkraut, and white parts of the scallions with 1.5 tbsp soy sauce and sesame oil until the mince is crispy. Set aside.
6. In the same pan, add a bit more oil and fry the garlic, ginger, and fresh chili for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
7. Add the sauce mixture to the pan, bring to a boil, and simmer for 2–3 minutes. Taste and adjust with more soy sauce, vinegar, or chili oil as needed.
8. To serve, either:
   - Place sauce in the bottom of each bowl, add noodles, then top with mince and green scallions, finishing with Sichuan pepper, or
   - Mix everything together in the pan, adding the noodles and mince, and serve topped with scallions and Sichuan pepper. Add a splash of water if needed to loosen.
9. Enjoy immediately! 🥢✨

## Notes

- For less heat, reduce or omit chili oil and Sichuan pepper.
- Udon noodles can be swapped for other thick wheat noodles.
- Sauerkraut adds tang and depth—don’t skip it!
- Great for lunch or dinner and meal prep.
