## Ingredients

| quantity | unit | key           | ingredient                        | notes           |
|----------|------|---------------|-----------------------------------|-----------------|
| 3        | dl   | chana-dal     | split chickpeas                   |                 |
| 8        | dl   | water         | water                             |                 |
| 0.25     | tsp  | turmeric      | turmeric                          |                 |
| 1        | tbsp | oil           | neutral oil                       | for tempering   |
| 1        | tsp  | cumin-seeds   | whole cumin seeds                 | or ground cumin |
| 1        |      | onion         | onion, finely chopped             |                 |
| 3        |      | garlic        | garlic cloves, finely chopped     |                 |
| 2        | tsp  | ginger        | fresh ginger, finely chopped      | or grated       |
| 0.5      | tsp  | turmeric      | turmeric                          | for tempering   |
| 0.5      | tsp  | chili-powder  | chili powder                      |                 |
| 0.5      | tsp  | coriander     | ground coriander                  |                 |
| 1        | tsp  | salt          | salt                              |                 |
| 2        |      | tomato        | tomatoes, finely chopped          | or 2 dl crushed |
|          |      | garam-masala  | garam masala                      | to serve        |
|          |      | roti          | roti or chapati                   | optional        |

## Instructions

1. Rinse the chana dal and soak for 30–60 minutes.
2. Add chana dal, water, and turmeric to a pot. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 30 minutes until the dal is soft.
3. Heat oil in a pan, fry cumin seeds until fragrant (if using ground cumin, add later). Add onion, garlic, and ginger, sauté until the onion softens.
4. Add turmeric, chili powder, coriander, and salt. Fry for a minute.
5. Add tomatoes and cook until they darken in color.
6. Stir the tomato mixture into the cooked dal and mix well. Simmer for another minute.
7. Serve in bowls, sprinkle with garam masala, and enjoy with roti or chapati if desired. 🥄✨

## Notes

- Chana dal is split chickpeas and gives a nutty, toothsome texture.
- For a richer dal, add a knob of vegan butter before serving.
- Adjust chili to taste for more or less heat.
- This dal is naturally vegan and perfect for cozy meals!
