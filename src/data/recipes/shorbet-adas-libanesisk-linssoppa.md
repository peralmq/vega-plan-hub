## Ingredients

- 4 dl yellow or red lentils (about 320 g), rinsed
- 1 dl rice (optional, or substitute with vermicelli)
- 1 liter water (plus more as needed)
- Salt or vegetable bouillon, to taste
- 1 yellow onion (or 1 dl crispy fried onion)
- Finely chopped parsley (optional, for garnish)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 0.5 tsp black pepper
- 2–3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- Oil for frying (if using fresh onion)
- [to serve] Lemon wedges 🍋
- [optional] Fried pita bread croutons

## Instructions

1. Rinse the lentils thoroughly until the water runs clear. Bring the lentils to a boil in 1 liter of water, skimming off any foam. Reduce to medium heat.
2. Rinse the rice and add it to the pot with the lentils. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. (If using vermicelli instead, add after blending the soup.)
3. Season with salt or bouillon and black pepper. Simmer covered for about 45 minutes, adding more water if needed.
4. If using vermicelli, blend the soup smooth first, then add the vermicelli and cook for 10 more minutes. If using rice, do not blend.
5. Finely chop the onion and fry in oil until deeply browned. (Or use crispy fried onion.)
6. Add the fried onion, lemon juice, and cumin to the soup. Simmer for 5 more minutes.
7. Garnish with parsley if desired. Serve hot with lemon wedges and optionally fried pita bread croutons. 🥣🍋

## Notes

- For a milder soup, use yellow lentils; for a richer color, use red lentils.
- The rice makes the soup extra creamy, but you can omit it or use vermicelli for a different texture.
- Fried pita bread is a classic topping for extra crunch.
- Adjust lemon and cumin to taste before serving.
- This soup is naturally vegan and perfect as a starter or light meal!
